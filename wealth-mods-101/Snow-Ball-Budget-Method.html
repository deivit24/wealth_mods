Does The Snowball Method Actually Work To Get Out Of Debt?

Hope vs Math

  • Wealth MODs 101
  • Does The Snowball Method Work?

Imagine you are on top of a hill. It’s the middle of winter, you roll up a snowball, and you let it roll down the hill.

As it’s rolling down, it starts picking up traction and momentum. Now it’s a huge snowball that demolishes anything that’s on its way. This is an excellent way to build snowballs but even a better way to pay your debt (that doesn’t include mortgage).

So, what is the Debt Snowball Method?


It may sound counterintuitive, especially for logical individuals, but this strategy involves paying off your smallest to largest debt. Here is the kicker; the interest rate doesn’t matter.

People that know a thing or two about interest rates will always tell you to start paying off the debt with the highest interest rate first. They’re not wrong, technically. More on that later.

The beauty of the snowball debt strategy is that it’s designed to help change psychological behaviors you may have with money. If you can force yourself to be intentional about paying one debt at a time until you’re free from that debt, it will give you control over your debt.

Are you starting to see the snowball reference? You demolish your smallest debt and move on to the next one.

Why does it work?


It’s not about the math behind it. It’s all about modifying your financial behavior. When you start eliminating debt, one by one, there is a sense of reward, and you be consistent and relentless in sticking to the strategy.

When I said logical people would say to pay off higher-interest debt first, I said they were technically not wrong. This is what it would look like if you were to pay off the higher debt first:




As you can see, you actually save more, and it takes the same amount of time. But it takes longer to eliminate the first debt. Although you see the numbers decrease in the balance sheet, it will be more likely that you stop using the strategy because you will have small debt lingering around and that could be annoying.

This is how many months it will take to eliminate each debt:

  • Credit Card – 6 months
  • Car Loan – 15 Months

As you can see, if you use the snowball debt reduction method, you eliminate your debt at a faster pace. In 15 months, you would eliminate three debt balances – as a pose to taking 26 months to eliminate three. You will be feeling a sense of accomplishment faster, and the probability of you sticking to the strategy will be higher.

I don't know if anyone knows a thing or two about credit scores, but the faster you can get a balance down to zero, the better.

Which Debt Should I Include?


You 'haven't left yet?

So… you really are thinking of adding this MOD into your financial life. Perfect

The debt that can be included is everything but your Mortgage and Credit Cards that you use every month in which you pay the full amount every month.

For example, you might have a credit card that you only used once for a huge purchase like a $2,000 computer or vacation, and that's it. That is the type of credit card debt that you can use.

Other examples of debt that you can use with the Snowball Debt Reduction Strategy:

  • Car Loans
  • Credit Card Balances
  • Personal Loans
  • Payday Loans - I don't know why on earth you would get this
  • Medical Bills
  • HELOC Loans
  • Student Loans

How do I start?


Easy. If you have one, sit down with your partner, and start writing down all the debt that you have in order from smallest to largest, regardless of interest. From there:

  • Make the minimum payments on all debt besides the smallest one
  • Pay down the smallest one as much as you can
  • Repeat the cycle until each debt is fully paid off
